To learn more about how we process your personal information, please contact us through our website.

2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that may be placed on your device when you interact with our website. They can help websites remember details about your visit, such as your preferred settings, which can enhance your experience on subsequent visits. Cookies can also be used for troubleshooting errors, understanding service usage, and supporting essential functionality.

Cookies set directly by us are called first-party cookies. We also use third-party cookies, which originate from a domain different from the one you are currently visiting. Similar technologies, such as pixels and local storage, can also be used for these purposes. For simplicity, we refer to these technologies as "cookies" in this policy.

Cookie Duration: Your cookie consent preferences are stored to remember your choice and avoid repeatedly asking for the same consent.

6. Managing Cookies

You can manage your cookie preferences through your browser settings. Your web browser may allow you to manage your cookie preferences, including to delete and disable cookies. You can take a look at the help section of your web browser or follow the links below to understand your options:

Browser Cookie Settings:
  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and site permissions

Important: Changes you make to your cookie settings may affect the availability or functionality of the website. Cookies listed as "Necessary" are required for the website to function and cannot be disabled.

Cookie settings are device- and browser-specific, so you will need to set cookie preferences for each device's browser.
